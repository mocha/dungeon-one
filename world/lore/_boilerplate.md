# The Sundering of the Elder Grove

<!-- SUMMARY: What is this? A one-paragraph overview
     for quick reference. -->
## Summary

Three centuries ago, a magical catastrophe split the original Elder Grove into fragments, scattering pockets of wild magic across [[The Western Reach]]. The event shaped the region's geography and is the origin of much of the area's unnatural wildlife.

<!-- DETAILS: The full account — as much or as little

## Details

The druids of the old Circle attempted a ritual to strengthen the wards protecting the Grove from an incursion of fiendish energy seeping up from deep underground. The ritual went wrong — whether through sabotage, miscalculation, or the strength of what they were fighting — and the resulting explosion of wild magic shattered the Grove into dozens of fragments.

The survivors, led by the druid who would become the first [[Archdruid Senna]] (the title has been passed down since), reformed as [[The Verdant Circle]] and dedicated themselves to protecting what remained.

## Notes

- The "thorn" corruption seen in creatures like the [[Thornback Warg]] is believed to be a lingering effect
- Some fragments of the original Grove still pulse with wild magic — these are sacred sites for [[The Verdant Circle]]
- The fiendish incursion was never fully sealed. Something is still down there.
