# Velundra, The Root Mother

<!-- DESCRIPTION: How is this deity perceived?
     What do they look like in art, visions, or myth? -->
## Description

Velundra is depicted as an ancient tree whose roots extend into the underworld and whose branches hold the sky. In more humanoid representations, she appears as an ageless woman whose lower body merges into a tangle of roots. She is both the force that makes things grow and the force that reclaims the dead.

<!-- TENETS & WORSHIP: What do followers believe?

## Tenets & Worship

Velundra's followers believe in the cycle — growth, harvest, decay, renewal. They don't mourn death as an ending but as a return to the root. Worship involves tending living things, composting the dead, and meditating in old-growth forests.

[[The Verdant Circle]] considers Velundra their patron, though not all Circle members are devout.

## Notes

- Her holy symbol is a circle of roots with a single leaf
- Temples are always living groves, never built structures
- Some scholars argue Velundra and [[The Decay]] (a feared entity from the deep) are two aspects of the same power
